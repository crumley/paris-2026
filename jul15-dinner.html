⏱️ Timing reality: a 5pm pre-cruise dinner doesn't work at any of these - Paris kitchens open for dinner around 7pm (the two bistros at 7:30). The clean plan is cruise ~7pm → dinner ~9pm. If you'd rather eat before, make it a late lunch (~1:30pm) instead.

🌾 Gluten note: none of these mark allergens or a "sans gluten" menu, so the ratings below are about how many dishes are naturally wheat-free (or easily adapted). Whichever you pick, call and flag it - and ask about shared fryers (frites) and the floured-fish station.

Quick read: to seat 7 at 9pm with workable gluten-free, Brasserie des Prés is the safest (180 seats). For the friendliest GF menu, Cinq-Mars - if you can squeeze 7 into its ~40 seats. L'Ami Jean shows a 21:00 slot for 6 - the open question is squeezing a 7th or splitting into two adjacent tables (it's famously tight). Big rooms flex; small bistros don't.

Booking tip for 7: the apps cap group size at 6, so a 7-top often shows "unavailable" even when the room has space - call instead, or book 6 and confirm the 7th by phone.
