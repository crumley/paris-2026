Relaxed, un-fancy dinner spots with a real table outside, plenty of atmosphere and people-watching, honest cooking, and a proper classic French wine list (real bottles, not the natural-wine scene). No tourist landmarks, no white-tablecloth formality. Bonus points for a short hop from the Palais Royal hotel. Tap a pin or a card for more.

🌿 Outside first, casual first. Honest note on geography: right by the hotel, the only proper open-air table is the Palais Royal garden terrace at Villalys (simple food, unbeatable setting), with Bistrot Vivienne's covered glass gallery as the sheltered fallback. The real casual-terrace payoff is a short métro east - the 11th, 12th, 6th, 7th and the Marais - where the lively neighborhood bistros are. A green 🌿 TERRACE tag marks genuine open-air seating. All of these take reservations; book the terrace tables ahead, especially the buzzy ones.
Near the hotel Casual terrace · short métro 🌿 Real open-air terrace Our hotel
If you only pick one: for the full casual-terrace evening, Le Square Trousseau (leafy 1907 terrace by the Aligre market) or Bistrot Paul Bert (the perfect classic-bistro night). For people-watching buzz, Chez Janou in the Marais. For a quieter hidden terrace, Le Bon Saint Pourçain. If the wine list is the point, Vantre. And two minutes from the door, Villalys on the garden is the low-effort open-air option. (Want something dressier with a terrace instead? Restaurant du Palais Royal's garden or Grand Cœur's Marais courtyard are the fancier fallbacks - just ask and I'll add them back.)
